# English Academic Style

## Core Principles

- Preserve technical meaning.
- Control claim strength.
- Use hedging when evidence is partial.
- Avoid promotional language.
- Separate observation, interpretation, implication, and hypothesis.

## Hedging Language

Useful expressions:

- may indicate
- suggests that
- is likely to
- under the tested conditions
- within the scope of this study
- the results are consistent with
- further validation is needed

Avoid unsupported expressions:

- proves that
- completely solves
- breakthrough
- revolutionary
- universally applicable
- dramatically improves, unless quantified and supported

## Claim Strength Control

- Use "significantly" only when statistical testing supports it.
- Use "improved by X%" only when the baseline, metric, and calculation are clear.
- Use "associated with" instead of causal wording when causal inference is not supported.
- Use "in this setting" or "under these conditions" when generalization is limited.

## Revision Checklist

- Does the sentence add any unsupported conclusion?
- Does the sentence remove important conditions?
- Does the sentence turn a correlation into a causal statement?
- Does the sentence imply broader validity than the experiment supports?
